Dan Marino

The best quarterback the Miami Dolphins ever had!

Daniel Constantine Marino Jr.

Played in the NFL for 17 seasons with the Miami Dolphins. He has held or current holds dozens of NFL records, despite never winning the Super Bowl. He is recognized among the greates quartebacks in American football history! He is Best remembered for his quick relase and powerful arm.

He is a nine-time Pro Bowl selection, six-time first or second team All-Pro, and All-AFC six times. Marino won all three major individual awards: NFL MVP (1984), NFL Comeback Player of the Year (1994), and NFL Man of the Year (1998).

When he finally retired, Marino held more than 40 NFL single-season and career passing recods!

History

  1. 1961

    Born September 15, 1961

  2. 1975-1978

    Attended Central Catholic High school in Pittsburg. He played baseball and football. in 1979 he was darfted in the 4th round by the Kansas City Royals but decided to play college football instead.

  3. 1979 - 1982

    Marino attended the University of Pittsburgh. His sophmore year (1980) Marino led the team to the No. 2 ranking! In 1981, he led the Panthers to win the Sugar Bowl with a game winning touchdown with less than a minute left in the game. Overall, during his first three years he led the team to 33 wins with only 3 losses. He finished his college career with 8,597 passing yards and 79 touchdowns.

  4. 1983

    Marino was drafted by the Miami Dolphins as the 27th pick in the first round. He would be the backup for the first 5 games but would get his first start against the Buffalo Bills in week 6. Marino would set several recods in as a rookie. He posted a 96.0 passer rating, he was selected to the Pro Bowl as a rookie, had the lowest percentage of passes intercepted, and was the only rookie to lead a conference in passing.

  5. 1984

    In his second year, Marino would go on to break six NFL full-season passing records, including the records for most touchsown passes and most passing yards. He would also be selected as the NFL's MVP He would lead he team to the Super Bowl against the San Francisco 49ers. Unfortunetly they would lose this game and this would be Marions only Super Bowl appearance.

  6. 1985

    Marino Would throw for 4,137 yards and 30 touchdowns, leading the Dolphins to the AFC Championshiip. He would again lead the league in completions, yards, touchdown passes and was named first-team All-Pro.

  7. 1986

    On September 7, he would throw his 100th touchdown pass in just 44 games. This is the fastest anyone has met this milestone. He would go on to throw for 4,746 yards with 44 touchdowns that season. He bacame the first QB in history to record three consecutive seasons with 30 or more touchdown passes. He would again be named first-team ALL-Pro.

  8. 1988

    This season Marino threw for 4,434 yards with 28 touchdowns. This would make him the first QB in history to throw for 4,000 or more yards in four differnet seasons.

  9. 1992

    Marino Would lead the Dolphins to the AFC Championship game. He would have 4,116 yards with 24 touchdowns this season. This would be the 5th time he led the league in passing yards.

  10. 1993

    While the Miami Dolphins were stongly favored to head back to the AFC Championship game and maybe the Super Bowl. Marino's season would be cut short in Cleveland when he tore his ACL.

  11. 1994

    Late in the season against the New York Jets, Marino would lead the team in a comeback win. He would execute a play known as 'The Clock Play' where he fake spiked the ball for the winning touchdown. he would be rewared the NFL's Comeback player of the Year. He would lead the team back to the playoffs where they would lose in a heartbreaking loss to the Charges in the AFC Divisonal round.

  12. 1999

    In Marino's final season he would lead the team back to the playoffs. His final win was in the playoffs against the Seattle Seahawks.

  13. 2000

    Dan Marino's jersey number 13 was retired and a life sized statue of Marino would be installed at Pro Player Stadium.

  14. 2003

    Marino was honored for his outstading carrer at Pitt. He was inducted into the College Football Hall of Fame.

  15. 2005

    Dan Marino is inducted into the NFL Hall of Fame. Where he threw 'one last pass' to former teammate Mark Clayton.

  16. 2014

    Announced August 24th that he was returning to the Miami Dolphins as a special advisor. He is currently still in this role.
